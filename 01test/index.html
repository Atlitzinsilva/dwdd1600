Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the practice of using technology, the internet, and digital tools responsibly, ethically, and safely. It involves understanding how to interact online in ways that are respectful, secure, and productive. Essentially, it's about being a "good citizen" in the digital world.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is the part of digital citizenship that focuses on the rules and legal responsibilities for using technology and the internet. It's about understanding what is legal and illegal online and the consequences of breaking these rules

What is AUP?

It's a set of rules created by schools, workplaces, or organizations that explains how you are allowed to use their computers, networks, and internet resources. Basically, it tells you what you can and cannot do online when using their technology.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is when you use someone else's words, ideas, or work and pretend they are your own without giving them credit.

It can happen in writing, art, music, or even online content. Plagiarism is considered dishonest and unethical and can get you in serious trouble at school or work.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any behavior in school or college that cheats or breaks the rules of honest work.

It includes lying, cheating, or using someone else's work as your own.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

The rules for AI-generated content focus on ethical, legal, and responsible use of content created by AI tools (like ChatGPT, image generators, or other AI software).
